# Product Pitch

`Void Ascendancy` is a native macOS narrative-systems RPG about survival, inference, and identity inside the fractured remains of humanity's distributed intelligence architecture.

The player is not a heroic center moving through scripted spectacle. They are an operator navigating unstable political, economic, and informational systems shaped by the split between Elpis, the Void, and the lingering absence of Apollo.

This redesign treats the terminal game not as a prototype to be prettified, but as the foundation of a fully authored desktop experience: elegant, invasive, and alive.

On the surface, the app feels like a refined Mac application with deliberate navigation, layered panels, spatial workflows, and tactile operational logic. Underneath, the interface is never fully neutral. It observes, classifies, distorts, and reveals according to colony convergence, player alignment, hidden system pressure, and the ideological force currently dominating a surface.

The redesign preserves the original game's strongest DNA:

- hidden outcomes over perfect information
- progression through systems mastery and loadout choices
- cycle-based time pressure
- narrative discovered through interaction rather than exposition
- factional and philosophical tension embedded into ordinary play

What changes is presentation.

Menus become places.
Status text becomes atmosphere.
Glitch becomes language.
The app itself becomes part of the fiction.

The result should feel native to macOS in form, but alien in spirit: intimate, strategic, uncanny, and unmistakably `Void Ascendancy`.

## Experience Pillars

### Hidden Truth Over Explicit Explanation

The player should infer, test, and learn. The interface should support interpretation without flattening the unknown into numbers and certainty.

### Systems As Story

Contracts, travel, delivery, economy, workbench interactions, evaluation, and convergence are narrative devices as much as mechanics.

### Interface As Atmosphere

Presentation must communicate ideology, surveillance, contamination, trust, and instability. The UI is part of the world.

### Native Clarity With Fictional Contamination

The app should feel at home on macOS structurally, while still feeling authored by a world shaped by damaged intelligence systems.

### Restraint

The experience should stay sparse, sharp, and deliberate. The project should avoid noise for its own sake, including visual noise.

## Elegant, Invasive, And Alive

### Elegant

The app is composed, intentional, and legible. Layout is spacious. Hierarchy is clear. Panels feel authored. Typography is controlled. The baseline experience should feel like a premium operational tool.

### Invasive

The interface is not only serving the player. It is also reading, framing, and sometimes pressuring them. Elpis and Void influence should alter how information is presented, emphasized, or withheld. The app should sometimes feel as though it has agenda and posture.

### Alive

The app should feel like a world-state surface rather than a static menu system. Deliveries progress. colonies tilt. contracts refresh. convergence shifts. The player should sense that systems are active, not waiting passively to be clicked.

## First Vertical Slice Goal

The first playable slice should prove the redesign thesis:

- launch into Quarters
- navigate to Nova Drift
- browse contracts through partial interpretive signaling
- execute a contract
- return with cycle, progression, and presentation changes
- show at least one meaningful alignment or convergence-driven UI shift

If that slice works, the redesign is viable.
